Literacy, or actually, the lack thereof, is Brazil’s most deep and lasting social issue.

Most Brazilians are illiterate in some level. Only 1 in 4 Brazilians are fully literate (math, reading and writing).

Everything we do is carefully and tenderly thought of to bring the passion for reading to Brazilian students in the most impoverished areas of the continental country.

By helping poor young students to see reading as a passion, as something enjoyable and fun, we are certain that we are sowing seeds that will blossom beautifully in the future.

FAQ

What does Holistica Foundation (HF) do?
Holistica Foundation is committed to transforming lives of Brazilian students through literacy. We do that by gifting students in impoverished areas of the country brand new literacy books.

We understand that education development involves complex socioeconomic issues and it is not restricted to having access to literary works. However, several international studies confirm the importance of reading for academic achievement. In an article published in the journalÂ Research in Social Stratification and MobilityÂ a group of sociologists linked to universities in Nevada and California, in the United States, presented the largest international study on the impact of reading on academic success. The main finding: the greater the number of literary works at home, the higher is the educational level (in years) of the people growing up in that home.

Then, what we do is to provide students an additional opportunity to grow.

Where in Brazil does HF work?
HF supports 1BOOK4LIFE Program, which was responsible for mapping all existing public schools in Brazil, taking into account social and geographical aspects and the Basic Educational Development Index (IDEB) in Brazil. Municipalities with more critical indicators were prioritized. At first, 2,3 million students in over 6,000 schools will benefit in the first stage of operations.
